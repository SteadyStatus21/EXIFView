How to use EXIFView

A note of privacy. I made this service that runs entirely in your browser. I will never see your images, as they are processed on your computer. This is a common misconception. More questions? Open an issue on GitHub.

Created by SteadyStatus21, with open-sourced dependencies

Loading Images from your PC (Downloads)

  • Ensure that you have the image downloaded or saved somewhere!
  • Open the tool (External Site), and select Choose File.
  • This will open a dialog, where you can select an image from your local computer.
  • Then, select that image from your computer, and now the text next to Choose File should be the file name you just inserted.
  • Select Load File and scroll down.
  • The important things to look for are:
    • ExposureTime - Shows Exposure Time in Seconds (ex. 1/1000)
    • FNumber - a.k.a. Aperture, shown in f-stop respectively. (ex. f/1.8)
    • ISOSpeedRatings - Shows ISO Rating from the Camera. (ex. 3200)
    • and,
    • ExposureMode - Shows whether the Camera was using Manual mode (“Manual exposure”) or Auto mode (“Auto exposure”).

Selecting an image from Google Drive (Chromebooks)

To do this, you need to understand that the Google Drive in the File Selector is in the left pane:

  • This process is mostly the same, where you need to click on Choose File, and navigate to the Google Drive tab, where it will let you navigate your Drive.
  • Once selected, select Open. Caution: This can take a second to load, especially for larger files!
  • Then, you can explore the EXIF info for that file.
